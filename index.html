CHPS is focused on the community-level of the health system where basic services are offered by Community Health Officers in village locations with a focus on maternal and child health. They are aided by volunteers who do health promotion work, engaging the community and visiting households. The broader community and its leaders are active members of the CHPS program, supporting the delivery of services, offering practical and financial support.

Dr Laud Boateng is the District Director in Nkwanta South. He is working for Ghana Health Services and managing the health services and associated research. Professor Margaret Kweku is a Senior Lecturer at the University of Health and Allied Sciences in Ho. She is leading the embedded research in Nkwanta South.

Ghana has a decentralised health system. District Directors are responsible for managing and implementing services based on national guidance, monitoring how successfully services are being rolled out and ensuring that targets are met. The implementation of the CHPS embedded research is also decentralised and the University of Health and Allied Sciences is responsible for planning and implementing the research in Nkwanta South with the support and guidance of colleagues at national level.

Ghana Health Services is responsible for implementing government policy in the health sector. They aim to increase access to services, with a focus on primary health care, and manage health sector resources, both human and financial. This includes the training of health care workers and creating technical guidelines for the delivery of services which are used at the district and community level. The University of Ghana is the largest university in the country and conducts both teaching and research. They lead on the evaluation of the embedded research.
